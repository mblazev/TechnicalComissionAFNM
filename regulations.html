ℹ️ For Competitors

All competitors participating in FIA CEZ events under AFNM are required to comply with the regulations listed on this page. In case of any conflict between documents, the FIA International Sporting Code takes precedence, followed by the CEZ Championship Regulations, followed by the Event Supplementary Regulations. When in doubt, contact the Technical Commission before the event.

Safety Equipment Requirements

⚠️ 2026 Safety Equipment Update

From 2026 onwards, all safety equipment must comply with current valid FIA rules as per the CEZ Championship Regulations. This includes helmets, HANS devices, race suits, gloves, and boots. Equipment with expired homologations will not be accepted at scrutineering.

🪖 Helmets

Must comply with one of the following standards:
— FIA 8860-2018 (top level)
— FIA 8860-2018-ABP
— FIA 8859-2015
— Snell SA2020 / SA2025
Homologation must be current and valid.

🦺 Race Suit / HANS

Race suit: FIA 8856-2018 minimum
HANS / FHR device: FIA 8858-2010 or 8858-2016
Gloves: FIA 8856-2018
Boots: FIA 8856-2018
Underwear: FIA 8856-2018 recommended

🚗 Roll Cage / Safety Nets

All vehicles must be equipped with a homologated roll cage or roll bar as required by their class regulations. Safety nets (window nets) are mandatory for circuit racing under CEZ regulations. Harnesses must comply with FIA 8853/98 or 8853-2016, within homologation period.

🔥 Fire Suppression

An onboard fire extinguisher or suppression system is required for all cars. Systems must comply with FIA 8865-2015 or equivalent. Plumbing must reach engine bay and cockpit. Activation must be accessible to both driver and marshals from outside.

Noise Limits

📢 Standard Noise Limit

The maximum permitted noise level for all cars is 100 dB(A) unless a specific class regulation states otherwise. Measurements are taken at 0.5 m distance at a 45° angle to the exhaust outlet at the specified RPM.

GT3 Cars — Appendix J Art. 257A Rule 314

GT3 cars must not exceed 110 dB(A) at 3,800 rpm, or at three-quarter maximum revs if this is lower. Measurement taken at 0.5 m at 45° to exhaust exit. All noise reduction measures must be permanent and must not be cancelled by exhaust gas pressure. No heat shielding in contact with the exhaust for thermal insulation is permitted unless specifically homologated.

Scrutineering Process

📋 What to Bring to Scrutineering

All competitors must present the following at administrative and technical checks:

— Valid FIA or national competition licence
— Completed entry form (with organiser signature if required)
— Vehicle log book / homologation papers
— All safety equipment (helmet, suit, HANS, harness)
— Vehicle in race-ready condition
— Any technical passport required by class regulations (e.g. F4, GT3/4)

⏱ Timing

Scrutineering schedules are published in the event-specific Supplementary Regulations and updated via bulletins on the relevant race page. Always check the official notice board and bulletin archive for the latest schedule.
